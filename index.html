Help Topics

SAGRR is a 501C3 non-profit, all volunteer organization, whose Mission is to rescue and re-home adoptable Golden Retrievers in Southern Arizona in safe, loving homes; thereby improving the lives of both the dog and adoptive family.

This web site enables authorized SAGRR volunteers to manage:

  • Membership
  • Golden Retrievers
  • Adoptive Families

The site employs role based security to enable volunteers to work within their assigned boundaries. Your role is set by a site Administrator when you sign up.

Site Organization

The site is divided into two main sections:

  • Action Required - this provides focus on the members, dogs, and applicants that require some attention
    • Active Dogs - manage the dogs that have not yet been settled in a permanent home
    • Active Applications - manage the adoptive families that have not yet been permanently matched to a Golden.
    • Dog Follow-ups - Watch for expiring vaccinations, upcoming vet visits, see which dogs are on Medical or Behavior hold
    • Membership Renewals - Watch for expiring memberships or new members
  • All Records - provides access to all members, dogs, and applicants primarily for historical purposes
    • Dogs - records for all the dogs grouped by status
    • Applications - records for all adoptive families grouped by status.
    • Members - records for all members grouped in a number of ways
    • Veterinarians - a list of all the vets that SAGRR has used
    • Teams & Interests - provides a simple way to look for members that have certain expressed interests, they can then be assigned to a team. There are two teams that must have at least one member:
      • Home Visit - Members on this team can be assigned to perform a home visit for new applicant's
      • Intake - Members on this team are responsible for new Golden's until they reach at least a temporary foster situation

Application Process Flow

Some of the application process flow is automated by this web site, but there are several steps that require volunteer action: (click here to download pdf of Applicant Flow)

  • Receive a new application - an application from an adoptive family is received by email or US mail.
  • Draft - a volunteer uses the "Start New Dog Intake" process in the "Active Dogs" section of this site, the application will stay in draft until the application entry is finished. Once the application is finished the site will place the application into the "New Request" state.
  • New Request - a volunteer will make arrangements for a volunteer to perform a Home Visit, they will then update the application status to move it "Schedule Home Visit" status.
  • Schedule Home Visit - the selected Home Visit volunteer will schedule and then perform a home visit. Once complete they will update the application to "Home Visit Complete" status.
  • Home Visit Complete - this is a holding status to allow a volunteer to ensure that all the necessary information has been collected to ensure that decision can be made on this application.
  • Decision Review - a volunteer with approval authority, will review the application and either approve or not approve. If approved it will be automatically moved to Approved Waitlist status
  • Approved WaitList - the application will stay in this status until the family is matched with a Golden or they withdraw, go inactive, or obtain a dog from another source (DFOS).
  • Approved ShortList - generally there are many more applicants than dogs, volunteers trying to find matches can place eager applicants into this status to allow them to focus on those that are ready now
  • Match Trial - when a possible match has been made, the site will place the applicant into this status
  • Matched Foster - when a foster or potential foster to adopt match has been made, the site will place the applicant into this status
  • Matched Adopt - when an adoption has been finalized, the site will place the applicant into this state and the record is marked complete.

There are a few exception states that applicants can be placed into

  • Application Withdrawn - an applicant indicated that they want to withdraw, the record will remain open for one year after which time it is closed and can't be re-opened.
  • Dog From Other Source - an applicant indicates that they found another dog, it is handled exactly like an application withdrawal.
  • Application on Hold - an application can be placed On Hold at anytime up through Decision. If there is something holding up the timely processing than it can be placed On Hold. When it comes off of hold it goes back to it's prior status.
  • Inactive - an approved application can be moved Inactive if they are not ready to be matched at this time. It will remain in the system indefinitely, when they are ready they are moved back to Approved Waitlist
  • Not Approved - a not approved application cannot be reopened.

Receiving a new Golden

Start the Intake process for a new golden by first clicking on "Active Dogs" and then click "Start New Dog Intake". Complete the initial intake form then click the Save button in the upper right corner. A record for the dog has now been created.

After hitting the Save button you will be on the newly created dog's record page. From here you can enter in Medical Information, Behaviors and Attributes, and any notes that you think pertinent.

You can also attach media to the record, so you can upload photos, scans, pdf, excel or word documents.

If you have a smart phone (iphone or Android) or tablet, you can use the camera to take a picture of the Golden for it's profile. If you don't, then you can always just upload a photo from your computer.

Adding a new adoptive family

Click on "Applications" then "Active", in the upper right corner is a "New Application" button. When you click it a new row is added to the top of the list, it will be LastName, FirstName. Click on this entry and you will be taken to the application record for this new adoptive family. You should see Application Incomplete! on the page.

First click on LastName, FirstName and enter the applicant's contact information and press Save and go Back.

Second click on Application Incomplete! and fill in all the sections of the application. You don't have to complete all sections in a single session, the application will remain in Draft until you can get back to it.

The site will detect when all application sections have been completed and will automatically move it from Draft to New Request

Adding a new member

There are two ways to create a member record:

  1. Create a member record from an applicant
  2. Manually add a member

Create Member Record from an Applicant

From the applicant page press either the "Create New" or the "Link Existing". Create New is used if the applicant is not a current member. Link Existing is used if the applicant is a current member, you will be presented with a list of all members, locate the associated member record and click on it.

Create Member Record Manually

Click on "Members" then "All Members", in the upper right corner is a "Add Member" button. When you click it a new row is added to the top of the list, it will be LastName, FirstName. Click on this entry and you will be taken to the member record for this new member.

First click on LastName, FirstName and enter the member's contact information and press Save and go Back.

Next click on Membership and fill in whatever information you know

You can now enter in Special Skills/Other Information, Company and Member's Interests

User Roles

By default, all users have read only access to the database. An administrator will assign user permissions based upon the role that the user will be performing. A user can have several permissions assigned.

    Dog – Dog records can be updated Members - Member records can be updated Applicant – Applicant records (applications) can be updated Approval – An applicant can be approved to receive a golden Dog Assignment – Can assign a dog to an applicant or member, also requires dog, member, and applicant permissions Administrator – Performs user and DB administration; accept new users, modify permissions, lock out users, enter Override mode to manually make status changes.
